GANDHI HALL

Mahatma Gandhi Hall located in Indore is considered to be one of the most beautiful buildings. Mahatma Gandhi Hall was constructed in 1904, it was earlier called King Edward Hall, but in 1948 it was changed to Mahatma Gandhi Hall. The hall is built in the Indo-Gothic style and was designed by Charles Frederick Stevens of Bombay. It is one of the famous landmarks of the city and is the most sought-after venue for grand public gatherings, exhibitions, fairs, etc. due to its huge capacity to accommodate around 2000 people. The complex also houses a library, a temple and a children's park.

Architecture of Gandhi Hall

Gandhi Hall was designed by Charles Frederick Stevens, its architectural style is Indo-Gothic. The grand monument is made of red stone, white marble and suture. Its majestic domes, high minarets and four-faced bell tower mesmerizes the people.

Interesting facts about Gandhi Hall

Located in the center of Gandhi Hall is a quadrangular clock tower- a type of tower that marks the landscape of every pre-colonial area of importance in India. Mahatma Gandhi Hall is generally used for cultural gatherings, arts and crafts exhibitions etc. This town hall is built in Indo Gothic style with local white and red stones. Its grounds are host to many local traditional and seasonal festivals and have murals depicting historical events inside.
